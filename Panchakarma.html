Panchakarma is the soul of ayurveda. As promised by ayurveda, Ayurveda believes in treating the disease at its root, not just symptomatic treatment and boosting the body’s capacity to resist and fight diseases. Medicines are very rarely used as props to help the body during sickness. Instead, the purpose of medicines here is to make the body capable of doing its natural function of fighting its own diseases. This is why you find that unlike some other systems of medicines, the disease does not recur when medication is stopped.

As the name suggests, Pancha in Sanskrit stands for Five and Karma are therapeutic measures thereby meaning five ways of body purification. These are undertaken for shodhan chikitsa - the purification of the body and mind, including the ten indriyas (sense and  work organs) Ayurveda considers it necessary before the start of any other therapy. In fact, most of the times, Panchakarma is an end in itself rather than a prelude to other therapeutic measures. During panchakarma, toxins from the body are removed by way of the skin and other glands, body vessels and the excretory system. 

In India, there are six seasons and panchakarma varies slightly according to these seasons. One can enjoy good health if routine seasonal panchakarma is done. (Prevention is better than cure)

The Panchakarma therapy of Ayurveda comprises of five basic types of advanced treatment for evacuation of vitiated Dosha (toxic materials) from the body. One can consider this as a cleansing program of toxins from the body. There are so many subtypes of this therapy and different types of herbal massages, fomentation's like steam, external oil treatments, Basti (medicated enemas), Virechana (purgation through herbs), Vamana (emesis through herbs), Nasya (nasal administration of oils) etc. are incorporated. These practices are extremely helpful in relieving deep seated diseases as well as it is also beneficial for maintaining and improving physical and mental health

Before starting with panchakarma, purvakarma (prekarma) are necessary. These include some medicines, snehana (oils and ghee), swedana (steam), etc.

Medicines help digestion and removal of aam dosha (toxins) from the body. Snehana is an important therapy in ayurveda for the treatment of various vata, pitta and kapha vyadhis. Snehana is of two types – internal and external. In the former, medicated oil or ghruta (ghee) is given to the patient for five to ten days (as per the person’s constitution and disease) early morning on an empty stomach. In the later, medicated oil is applied on the body and a proper massage is given. After massage,  swedana (steam) of medicinal herbs is given to the body so that the dosha is removed through the pores of the skin.

These purvakarmas are also useful independently in certain diseases. (for example, Snehana is useful to treat acidity, peptic ulcers, arthritis, ophthalmic problems, dryness of skin, skin disorders, problems due to heavy physical / mental work, excessive sexual activity, alcoholism, old age debility, anaemia, etc. Similarly, Swedana is useful against allergic rhinitis, cough, hiccoughs, bronchitis, body ache, headache, cervical spondylitis, Paraplegia, arthritis, low back pain, vertebral disorders, pain in any part of the body, piles, fistulae, facial palsy, problems after delivery and for weight reduction.)
            The result of prekarma (how patient feels) is very important for the doctor to decide the further course of action during the actual panchakarma.
